Methodologies Reviewed

1. DetectGPT (Reactive Detection)

  • Statistical fingerprinting of AI-generated text using probability curvature analysis
  • ‚úì Strength: No training data needed
  • ‚úó Weakness: Adversarial evasion, arms race

2. CAPTCHA (Challenge-Response)

  • Cognitive-perceptual human tests
  • ‚úì Strength: Simple, widely deployed
  • ‚úó Weakness: High friction, accessibility issues

3. Worldcoin Iris Scanning (Biometric)

  • Custom "Orb" hardware captures iris patterns with zero-knowledge proof claims
  • ‚úì Strength: High accuracy, AI-safe
  • ‚úó Weakness: Privacy violations, coercive incentives

4. Behavioral Biometrics (Passive Auth)

  • Typing rhythm, mouse patterns, touchscreen pressure - continuous monitoring
  • ‚úì Strength: Seamless, invisible to user
  • ‚úó Weakness: Total surveillance, sub-perceptual control

The Trilemma (Core Contribution)

Usability-Verification-Freewill Trilemma

Pick Any Two:

Verification + Freewill = Poor Usability (CAPTCHA, PGP)
Verification + Usability = Lost Freewill (Biometrics, Behavioral)
Usability + Freewill = No Verification (Pre-verification internet)
EMPIRICAL VALIDATION: Worldcoin pursued Verification + Usability
Philippines NPC ruled: Invalid consent, exploits vulnerabilities
Legal shutdown = Freewill protection via regulatory friction

Future Scope

Gap 1: Usable Privacy-Preserving Frameworks

  • Challenge: Systems are either usable-but-surveillant OR private-but-clunky
  • Need: HCI research on Self-Sovereign Identity + Content Provenance
  • Goal: Accessibility without chilling effects or social credit systems

Gap 2: Algorithmic-Age Consent Frameworks

  • Challenge: 20th-century consent laws inadequate
  • Need: Legal-technical frameworks for sub-perceptual identification
  • Question: How to give meaningful consent when algorithms operate below perception?

Gap 3: Collaboration as Differentiation

  • Challenge: Current focus on building walls between humans and AI
  • Need: Study Human-in-the-loop processes as locus of differentiation
  • Shift: From static verification to dynamic collaborative augmentation
